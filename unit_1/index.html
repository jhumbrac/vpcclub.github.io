Victoria Police Cricket Club

The Victoria Police Cricket Club (VPCC) was formed in 1977 and became incorporated in 1981. The Club is affiliated with the Victoria Police Amateur Sports and Welfare Society, who play a major role in ensuring the financial success of the Club.

The Club was primarily established to compete in the Australian Police Cricket Carnival, which is now known as the Australia and New Zealand Police Cricket Championships.

These days, a major function of the club is to participate in a number of Community Engagement events aimed at improving the relationship between Victoria Police and the wider community of Victoria through cricket.
